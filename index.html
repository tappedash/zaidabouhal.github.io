→ The Origin Story

Age 7: When It Started (2005)

I was 7 years old when I got my first computer. That's when everything began.

I played video games—learned that systems have logic. Input → Processing → Output. Rules create experiences. Actions have consequences.

Then came the internet: MSN Messenger, Myspace, early Facebook, YouTube in its first years, torrents, online forums. I watched people build digital communities. Connect. Share. Create. I was mesmerized by how this worked.

I asked myself: How do I build these things?

2013: The Creative Period

I was 15. At an age when you're discovering who you are.

I started making memes and joining Facebook meme communities. It sounds trivial now, but it taught me something crucial: content resonates. Humor spreads. Communities form around shared ideas. What makes something go viral? What keeps people engaged?

Around the same time, I started producing electronic music (EDM). Using tools like FL Studio, Ableton, synthesizers. Playing with sounds, layers, effects, timing, release. Watching tutorials. Understanding how software works from the inside out.

That's when it clicked: Software is just a tool. But the tools themselves are built by engineers. To make music, I need to understand the DAW (Digital Audio Workstation). To make memes go viral, I need to understand social platforms. To build anything, I need to understand systems.
